# 🧭 Project Lead Compensation & Values

> This page is about transparency — not just about money, but about the philosophy behind how this project is led. Anyone contributing, donating, or simply curious deserves to understand exactly how the person at the helm is compensated and why.

---

## Who This Page Is For

This page exists so that **every contributor, donor, and community member** can see:

- What the Project Lead earns
- Under what conditions they earn it
- Why the structure is designed this way

No hidden arrangements. No ambiguity.

---

## 🧱 The Core Value: Fair Share

This project is built on one foundational belief:

> **The Lead does not eat the whole pie.**

Leadership here is a service role — not a privileged one. The person guiding this project is not automatically entitled to more than those doing hands-on, high-impact work. A contributor who ships a critical security fix, refactors core architecture, or writes documentation that unblocks dozens of people may create more value than hours of oversight ever could.

The compensation model reflects this. Contributors can — and should — earn more than the Lead if their work warrants it. That's not a bug. That's the point.

---

## 💼 Project Lead Compensation

### Rate

| Type | Rate | Cap |
|------|------|-----|
| Hourly (when funded) | **$50 / hr** | **Capped per time needed**  |
| Unfunded periods | $0 — voluntary effort | No cap on contribution |

### Hours Cap

The Project Lead's hours are capped each period to prevent the fund from being disproportionately consumed by a single role. The exact cap is set transparently at the start of each funding cycle and is visible on the [HCB Ledger](https://hcb.hackclub.com).

This means:

- **If funding is sufficient** → the Lead works paid hours up to the cap, then continues voluntarily as needed.
- **If funding is limited or paused** → the Lead works on pure motivation and commitment. No fund is drained to sustain the Lead at the expense of contributor bounties.

### What the Lead's Hours Cover

Time logged by the Lead is eligible only for the following responsibilities:

- Strategic roadmap planning and prioritization
- PR review and code quality oversight
- Community management and mentoring junior contributors
- Security monitoring and server maintenance decisions
- Coordination with fiscal sponsor (HCB) and donors

Time spent on feature development, bug fixes, or tasks that qualify as bounty-eligible work will be treated as **bounty claims**, not hourly maintenance — even if done by the Lead. The Lead is not exempt from the bounty system.

---

## 🔋 Funding Tiers & Lead Behavior

| Fund Status | Lead Behavior |
|-------------|---------------|
| 🟢 **Healthy** | Full capped hours apply. Bounties active at all tiers. |
| 🟡 **Limited** | Lead hours reduced or paused first. Tier 1 bounties prioritized. |
| 🔴 **Paused** | Lead works voluntarily. No maintenance pay drawn. Bounties reviewed case-by-case. |

The Lead will not draw compensation when doing so would jeopardize contributor payouts. Bounties come first.

## 📣 Why This Is Public

Every payment from this project's fund — including payments to the Lead — is publicly visible through HCB's transparency dashboard. This page exists to go one step further: not just *showing* the numbers, but *explaining the reasoning* behind them.

If you are a donor, you deserve to know your contribution isn't being funneled disproportionately to one person.

If you are a contributor, you deserve to know the system isn't rigged in the Lead's favor.

NOTE: If you are the Lead reading this in the future — this page is a commitment, not just a policy.

---

## ⚖️ Summary of Principles

1. **Flat ceiling, not a floor.** The Lead has a fixed rate and a hard cap. There is no bonus, no override, no exception.
2. **Contributors can earn more.** If your work justifies it, it will be paid. Seniority of the Lead is irrelevant to your bounty.
3. **Voluntary muscle when funds run dry.** The project does not stop. The Lead does not disappear. The work continues — unpaid if necessary.
4. **No hidden arrangements.** If something changes (rate, cap, policy), it will be updated here and announced officially.
5. **Bounties before salary.** In constrained funding situations, contributor bounties take priority over Lead compensation.
